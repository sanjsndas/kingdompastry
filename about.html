Kingdom Pastry Orchard is a living journal — part orchard, part pastry lab, part love letter to heirloom fruit and the hands that transform it.

🌿 Our manifesto

We believe that the best pastries are born from a deep respect for the fruit — its variety, its season, its quirks. Our kitchen is a bridge between the wild orchard and the refined table.

🍑

Heirloom first

We work with forgotten varieties — apples with tannin, plums with acidity, pears with perfume. Flavor is our compass.

🔬

Science & soul

We measure, test, and document. But we also listen to the dough, feel the butter, and trust the fermentation.

📖

Storytelling

Every recipe carries a memory. We share the narratives behind the ingredients — the growers, the seasons, the discoveries.

👩‍🌾 The orchardists

A small crew of bakers, foragers, and writers who tend to this kingdom.

Margot Rhys

Founder · Pastry Scholar

Margot left a career in food science to pursue the poetry of pastry. She leads our fermentation and lamination studies.

Elias Voss

Head Baker · Grain whisperer

Elias sources ancient grains and mills them on-site. His rye-buckwheat blend is the backbone of our tarts.

Iris Bramble

Orchard keeper · Forager

Iris tends to our heirloom fruit trees and wild hedgerows. She writes about the seasons and the stories they hold.

⏳ Orchard chronicles

2020

Planting the seeds — Margot and Elias transform a derelict orchard into a pastry laboratory. First experimental galette: quince and honey.

2022

The fermentation shift — We begin documenting lacto-fermented fruit syrups and their use in glazes. The blog is born.

2024

A kingdom of flavour — Our heirloom apple collection reaches 22 varieties. We publish our first deep-dive on tannin in pastry.

2026

Present day — A growing archive of recipes, science, and stories. We continue to explore the intersection of fruit and flour.
